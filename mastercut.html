What are Laboratory Grown HPHT Diamonds?

These are diamonds grown in a controlled laboratory, wherein a team of gemologists and scientists strive to replicate natural processes of diamond making which otherwise takes millions of years to form under the ground. HPHT stands for high pressure, high temperature and is one of the primary method used to grow diamonds in a lab. This diamond growth process subjects carbon to extreme temperatures and pressures and is meant to replicate the extreme heat and pressure conditions deep within the earth where natural diamonds form.

Are these the same as imitations?

Some people might refer to lab-grown diamonds as imitations or simulants, but this is incorrect. Actual imitations like cubic zirconia or synthetic moissanite — which only look like diamond — have very different chemical and physical properties that allow trained gemologists to identify them easily. However, lab-grown diamonds are more challenging to detect

Are these the same as imitations?

One cannot differentiate between a Natural Diamond and an HPHT diamond. They look exactly the same with exact amount of shine. Laboratory-grown diamonds are essentially chemically and optically the same as their natural counterparts, traditional gemological observations and old-style “diamond detectors” are not able to tell them apart. Identification at a professional gemological laboratory or using sophisticated device developed by GIA and other organisations are the only reliable methods to separate the from natural diamonds.
